About Project

Our mission is to construct a website that offers several products with style and ease to help with the current COVID-19 pandemic. With the current pandemic, there has been a limited supply of gear and products that are tailored for fashion and have limited fashion and aesthetic appeal. For example, all the PPP blue masks might not look great on an individual’s outfit, or it looks out of match for the color pattern they are wearing. With our website, we aim to extend the reach of products that are aimed to product individuals from COVID as well as look and feel aesthetically pleasing to the customer. This page is a collective of products and services that are tailored for COVID, as well as make the customer want to keep showing off their newest gear to their friends and family. We aim to deliver all our important products for all age groups, so that everyone can be a part of the fun. From masks, to face shields, to hand sanitizer, everything is crafted from the most high quality material in the market. High quality products at an affordable price, is what we aim to achieve with our webpage, and with easy accessibility as well. The user will be able to see from the moment they enter the homepage where they can go and buy their essential COVID items, and at what cost and deals they can obtain. Our page brings the stress of Covid pandemic to a halt, which can help boost usage of the website.

As this project is a final project for our CPI310 Web-Based Information Management System class in Arizona State University, we are planning to finish it by the end of Fall2020 semester, or within seven weeks. The deliverables of this project will include back-end and front-end elements of the website that allow users to do a full transaction on our website. Finally, our team is composed of four informative students and one digital culture student where we will divide our roles as front-end developers, back-end developers, a database administrator, and a project manager.

Use Case analysis

Our use case diagram utilizes a relationship between the customer and admin which is the employee. Since, we encourage customers to sell their own products such as masks, health kits, clothes, and other products that provide a safe environment. The customer role is to be a part of the system which is to buy products or sell their own products. The admin will sell company products and manage the customer’s products.

The customer can sell a product but must go through an admin. The customer fill out a form and gives an amount of time the product can be shipped. The admin will decide if the customer’s product is considered a Co-Vid safe product. If the admin deems to be not a product that fits the website goal, then the admin will send a message to the customer saying, “This product is not Co-Vidly Safe”.

When the customer is shopping, they can add products to their cart and delete products in their cart. When the customer is done shopping, then they can click on the cart icon and review the products in their cart. Then they can click on proceed to checkout and start purchasing the products. The customer will choose how to pay for their products by electronically paying for the products. They can pay by PayPal, add a credit card or debit card information, or others that involve their bank. The admin will set the shipping tax and date when adding a product. When the customer goes through the checkout process, then the products will be shipped to their address.

Database Design

The database design is pretty simple for an e-commerce website. The website needs to record the Customers, Products, Orders, and Order Details. From the customer, what is stored is the full name, date of birth, location, email, username, and password. This is by creating an ID for that customer to purchase or sell products on this website. The customer will be in our database when they register for the website. The products will be stored when an admin fills in a form that will be linked to our database. The admin also needs to input how much of the product there is in the warehouse and suggest a discount. If the customer wants to sell their products, they must fill out a form that will be sent to the admin. When a customer orders a product on our website, the database needs an ID to create an order which will have a shipping date for our admin’s to see.

Project Plan

Our project management will follow the agile methodology. Every week we will have a one-hour scrum meeting (every Tuesday at 8 pm) to talk about what we have done the previous week and what we are going to do for the following week. Besides that, we divide the project into modules, thus every week we will have something to deliver. The distribution of the modules throughout the semester will be as follows:

  • Week1 (10/12 – 10/17) - Finish “About Us” tab (proposal and pitch)
  • Week2 (10/19 – 10/24) - Research and design details for layouts and features
  • Week3 (10/26 – 10/31) - Create products listing page:
    • Front-end: Create the UI based on the layouts agreed on
    • Back-end: Program product searching and sorting feature
    • Database: Create template for products data
  • Week4 (11/2 – 11/7) - Create buyers’ account features:
    • Front-end: Create the UI based on the layouts agreed on
    • Back-end: Program functionalities to create an account (sign up), access an account (log in), and modifying shopping lists (adding, deleting, updating products in the cart)
    • Database: Create template for users data
  • Week5 (11/9 – 11/14) - Create transaction page:
    • Front-end: Create the UI based on the layouts agreed on
    • Back-end: Program functionalities to do transactions (payment) and send an email notification to the buyer and seller
    • Database: Create template for users data
  • Week6 (11/16 – 11/21) - Create sellers’ account features:
    • Front-end: Create the UI based on the layouts agreed on
    • Back-end: Program functionalities to create an account (sign up), access an account (log in), and modifying products listing (adding, deleting, updating products the sellers sell).
    • Database: Expand the template for users data
  • Week6 (11/16 – 11/21) - Create sellers’ account features:
    • Front-end: Create the UI based on the layouts agreed on
    • Back-end: Program functionalities to create an account (sign up), access an account (log in), and modifying products listing (adding, deleting, updating products the sellers sell).
    • Database: Expand the template for users data
  • Week7 (11/23 – 11/28) - Polishing and debugging before submission and Finalizing design document
