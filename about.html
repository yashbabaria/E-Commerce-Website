About Project

Our mission is to construct a website that offers products that are categorized as COVID-19 pandemic essentials effiently while adding some fashion to it. While there are many businesseses or e-commerce web apps that provide those essentials, it is hard to find one that is fashionable. For example, all the PPP blue masks might not look great on an individual’s outfit, or it looks out of match for the color pattern they are wearing. With our website, we aim to expand the market to the fashion industry. Besides that, we want to provide a collection of products and services that contribute in solving the products distribution issue during the pandemic, as well as in helping our customers to create their fashion statements for the new trend that will occur due to the world problem. The products that we develop will be suitable for all age groups, as we want to be inclusive and allows everyone to be a part of the fun no matter who they are. From masks to face shields and hand sanitizer, everything is crafted from the most high quality materials in the market. High quality products at an affordable price and asy accessibility is what we strive to provide with our business. The customers will be able to see our goal from the moment they enter our welcome page until they buy and receive the products we sell. Finally, we hopd our app can bring the stress of looking for COVID essential products to a halt by giving our customers the joy of shopping at our online store.

As this project is a final project for our CPI310 Web-Based Information Management System class in Arizona State University, we are planning to finish it by the end of Fall2020 semester, or within seven weeks. The deliverables of this project will include back-end and front-end elements of the website that allow users to do a full transaction on our web app. Finally, our team is composed of four informatics students and one digital culture student where we divide our roles as front-end developers, back-end developers, a database administrator, and a project manager.

Specifications

Purpose of Project (Motivations)

The purpose of this project is to create an e-commerce website that provides a platform for people to buy and sell COVID essentials easily. Since 2020 has been a tough year for everyone, our team wants to easen the tasks that people have to do due to the pandemic by bringing safety and style at the same time. Our project aims to encourage people to keep each other safe by providing products that are crucial during the pandemic while adding some fun and aestheticity to it. We expect our products to motivate our customers to show off their looks and care for the others' health simultaneously. The brand COVIDLY-Safe is creted to share the sense of a caring community, and our target market will be everyone affected by the pandemic. Safety and social distancing are critical during a pandemic, and we want everyone to be able to prioritize them whenever and wherever. For instance, we will provide fashionable products for the four different seasons: spring, summer, fall, and winter. No matter what the situations are, we want to see people caring for each other well by providing them with the tools or essentials that they need to be able to do so.

Use Case analysis

Our use case diagram utilizes a relationship between customers and admins or the web app providers. The customer role includes the buyers and the re-sellers or those who sell their own products.

The customer can sell their product, but the product must be checked by the admin first. The seller then will fill out a form and give the amount of time the product will be shipped. The admin will decide if the product is considered a COVID essential product. If the admin deems it to be a product that does not fit the business' purpose, then the admin will send a message to the customer saying, “I am sorry, but your product is not COVIDLY-Safe”.

When the customer is shopping, they can add products to their cart or delete them from their cart. Once the customer is done shopping, they can click on the cart icon and review the products in their cart. Then they can proceed to checkout and create a transaction for the products. The customer will choose how to pay their products online, such as by PayPal, a credit card or a debit card, a gift card, or a check. The admin will calculate the tax for the transaction. After the customer go through the checkout process, then the products will be shipped to their address.

Database Design

For the database design the web app needs to record the Customers, Products, Orders, and Order Details. In the Customers' database, the data that will be recorded are their full name, date of birth, location, email, username, and password. The data will be gathered when the customer creates their account to purchase or sell products on the app. The data or information in the Products database are stored when an admin fills out a form of the product to sell. If the customer wants to sell their products, they must fill out a form that will be sent to the admin. When a customer orders a product on our web app, the database needs the customer's ID to create the order.

Project Plan

Our project management will follow the agile methodology. Every week we will have a one-hour scrum meeting (every Tuesday at 8 pm) to talk about what we have done the previous week and what we are going to do for the following week. Besides that, we divide the project into modules, thus every week we will have something to deliver. The distribution of the modules throughout the semester will be as follows:

  • Week1 (10/12 – 10/17) - Finish “About Us” tab (proposal and pitch)
  • Week2 (10/19 – 10/24) - Research and design details for layouts and features
  • Week3 (10/26 – 10/31) - Create products listing page:
    • Front-end: Create the UI based on the layouts agreed on
    • Back-end: Program product searching and sorting feature
    • Database: Create template for products data
  • Week4 (11/2 – 11/7) - Create buyers’ account features:
    • Front-end: Create the UI based on the layouts agreed on
    • Back-end: Program functionalities to create an account (sign up), access an account (log in), and modifying shopping lists (adding, deleting, updating products in the cart)
    • Database: Create template for users data
  • Week5 (11/9 – 11/14) - Create transaction page:
    • Front-end: Create the UI based on the layouts agreed on
    • Back-end: Program functionalities to do transactions (payment) and send an email notification to the buyer and seller
    • Database: Create template for users data
  • Week6 (11/16 – 11/21) - Create sellers’ account features:
    • Front-end: Create the UI based on the layouts agreed on
    • Back-end: Program functionalities to create an account (sign up), access an account (log in), and modifying products listing (adding, deleting, updating products the sellers sell).
    • Database: Expand the template for users data
  • Week7 (11/23 – 11/28) - Polishing and debugging before submission and Finalizing design document

    • Features that may be added if time permits:
  • Adding security plug-ins for users’ accounts and transaction
  • Using cloud services to personalize the web app
      • Different accounts will show different products based on users’ search and transaction history
    Features that may be lost if time limits:
  • Seller accounts (because we are can provide our own products)
